Cu+ based active sites of different oxides supported Pd-Cu catalysts and electrolytic in-situ H2 evolution for high-efficiency nitrate reduction reaction
 Pd-Cu are loaded on various oxides supports and combine with electrolytic in-situ H2 generation for NO3R， which be confirmed an promising combined method to fulfill converting NO3- to N2. And different supported materials demonstrates huge NO3R rate and N2 selectivity disparity. Combined with experimentals and DFT calculation, Cu+, which generated on γ-Al2O3 and TiO2 surface,play the vital role for effective NO3− adsorption and high N2 selectivity.
*Author: C. Chen, H. Zhang, K. Li, Q. Tang, X. Bian, J.n. Gu, Q. Cao, L. Zhong, C.K. Russell, M. Fan, J. Jia X. Bian, T. Sun, J. Jia
*Journal of Catalysis, 392 (2020) 231-243.

 

Combination of Pd–Cu Catalysis and Electrolytic H2 Evolution for Selective Nitrate Reduction Using Protonated Polypyrrole as a Cathode
 Pd–Cu catalysis is combined with in situ electrolytic H2 evolution for NO3– reduction with protonated polypyrrole (PPy) as a cathode.Both the PPy film and Pd–Cu catalyst exhibit good stability and there is no Cu2+ or Pd2+ in solution after reaction. Since PPy is exclusive for NO3– adsorption and thus inhibits NO3– reduction. The Pd–Cu(4:1) exhibit the highest NO3– removal (100%) and N2 selectivity (93–95%) after 90 min.
*Author: C. Chen, K. Li, C. Li, T. Sun, J. Jia
*Environmental Science & Technology, 53 (2019), 13868-13877.

 

Co3O4 rod and sheet-like films with different crystal facet exposure and Co2+/Co3+ ratio are synthesized on Ti substrate through a facile hydrothermal method with the adjusting of hydrothermal pressure and duration. Low hydrothermal pressure is likely to obtain rod-like film with almost pure (220) facet exposure, while the sheet-like film with the mixture of (220) and (222) facets is formed with relatively high pressure. The films are employed as cathodes for electrocatalytic NO3−-N reduction. Better conversion of NO3−-N to NH4+-N is obtained by Co3O4 sheets film. The larger exposure of (220) facet and the more amount of Co2+ and oxygen vacancies result in the better NO3−-N removal and NH4+-N conversion kinetics of Co3O4 rods film than that of Co3O4 sheets.
*Author: K. Li, C. Chen, X. Bian, T. Sun, J. Jia
*Electrochimica Acta, (2020) 137121.
Electrolytic nitrate reduction using Co3O4 rod-like and sheet-like cathodes with the control of (220) facet exposure and Co2+/Co3+ ratio

 

Different phase structured MnO2, including β-, α-, γ- and three kinds of δ-MnO2, are prepared and their catalytic activities are compared through catalytic oxidation of toluene at different temperature. Toluene conversion results show that catalytic oxidation activity follows the sequence of β- < α- < γ- < δ-MnO2. When MnO2 owns the same δ phase structure, the crystallinity degree and morphology also affect toluene catalytic oxidation activity, but the effect is not so significant as that of phase structure. The results are further explained by The weakest toluene catalytic oxidation activity of β-MnO2 mainly results from its extreme small surface area. In comparison with γ- and δ-MnO2, the less amounts of Mn4+ and lattice oxygen on α-MnO2 surface result in its weaker catalytic oxidation activity. The special hierarchical structure of δ-MnO2 makes toluene more likely to be adsorbed on its surface, and thus the catalytic oxidation activity is better than that of γ-MnO2.
*Author: K. Li, C. Chen, C. Chen, H. Zhang, X. Hu, T. Sun, J. Jia
*Applied Surface Science, 496 (2019) 143662.
Effects of phase structure of MnO2 and morphology of δ-MnO2 on toluene catalytic oxidation
